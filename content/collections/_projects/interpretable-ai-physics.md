## Overview

AI systems can generate useful scientific structures, but those structures only become science when researchers can understand, test, and generalize them. This theme focuses on making AI outputs more interpretable for physics, especially when solutions are represented as graphs, optical layouts, or high-dimensional search results.

## Focus

- explaining machine-discovered physical structures
- linking abstract graph representations to laboratory concepts
- evaluating when an AI proposal is physically meaningful
- designing workflows where human intuition and machine search reinforce each other
